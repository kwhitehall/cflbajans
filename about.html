Mission Statement

The mission of the Coalition for the Barbados Association of Central Florida (CBAOCF), otherwise known as Central Florida Bajans (CFL Bajans), is to encourage networking and to promote kinship among Barbadians (Bajans) living in Central Florida. Our association maintains avenues of communication between Barbadians in Central Florida and their homeland Barbados, and fosters educational, cultural, and business operations, to further the joint interests of Bajans in both regions in an open, friendly environment.

Who we are

The Coalition for the Barbados Association of Central Florida (CBAOCF), otherwise known as the Central Florida Bajans (CFL Bajans), started in 1991, and currently has a membership affiliation of approximately 150 individuals. Over the past 26 years, we have made significant contributions to the local Central Florida community, and to charities, individuals, and schools in Barbados. This includes our adoption and support of the St. Lucy District Hospital in Barbados, and the offering of an annual scholarship to a worthy student of Bajan (Barbadian) heritage who resides in our local community.

What we do

The CFL Bajans promote kinship amongst Barbadians living in Central Florida, and serve as a resource for Bajans integrating into American culture.

Connect Barbadians in Central FL

Through encouraging networking and promoting kinship amongst Barbadians living in Central Florida, and the greater Florida area.

Connect Barbadians with the Florida community

Through community service and charitable events in our local community. Our philanthropic efforts are facilitated through group volunteer activities.

Connect with the Barbadian diaspora

Through participation in national and international events hosted by other Barbadian associations, and charitable contributions in Barbados.

Support Bajans (Barbadians) in their education quests

Through the provision of scholarships for high school graduates pursuing a degree at recognized higher education institution.

Showcase Bajan (Barbadian) and Caribbean culture

Through hosting events, and participating in Caribbean events hosted by other associations thus growing the diversity in Central Florida.

Where we are from

The CFL Bajans have roots in the beautiful island of Barbados.

Food!

Food is a big, BIG part of Barbadian culture, even BuzzFeed knows it! Bajan cuisine includes a unique blend of foods with African, Indian and British influences. The national dish is Cou-cou and flying fish. Cou-cou is made from cornmeal and okras. Although the national dish pairs it with flying fish in a tomato-based sauce, it is also commonly served with salt fish, smoked red herring, or stew.
Want to learn how to make Cou cou or other delicious Bajan dishes? Stay tuned for recipes to Barbadian delicacies from the CFL Bajans members and more!

Barbadian Fun Facts!

  • Barbados is the most easterly island in the Caribbean Sea.
  • The island is named after the Bearded Fig Tree once found in abundance on the island.
  • A Bajan is a person from Barbados - officially a Barbadian.
  • Barbados only flew the British flag until November 30th 1966 when independence was gained.
  • Superstar Rihanna is from Barbados.
  • The national sport of Barbados is cricket.
